ELEPHANTS

General Elephant Information - Knysna Elephant Park Elephants, the largest land mammals, are known for their massive size, distinctive features like long trunks, and large ears. They are grayish to brown in color with sparse, coarse body hair, and have tough, leathery skin. African and Asian elephants are the two surviving species. Key Features: Trunk: An elongated upper lip and nose, used for a variety of functions like lifting objects, grabbing food, and communicating. Ears: Large, flat ears that help regulate body temperature, especially in African elephants. Tusks: Enlarged incisor teeth made of ivory.

Skin: Thick, wrinkled, and not very hairy, protecting them from most other animals. Legs: Columnar, providing strong support for their massive size. Size: African elephants are larger than Asian elephants. Behavior and Social Structure: Elephants are highly

intelligent and have strong family bonds, with females leading tightly bonded families. They communicate using a variety of low sounds, some of which are inaudible to humans. Elephants are primarily herbivores, spending much of their day eating.

Threats: Elephants face threats from human encroachment, habitat loss, poaching for ivory, and human-elephant conflicts.
